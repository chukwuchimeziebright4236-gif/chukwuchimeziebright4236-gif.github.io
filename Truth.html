Truth

What is Truth?

Truth is a state completely void of deception. It is straight, unbending, and uncompromising in both principle and character.

Satan is a deceiver because it is his very nature to deceive. When Jesus spoke to the Jews who refused to believe the truth He was telling them, He declared in John 8:44:

“Ye are of your father the devil, and the lusts of your father ye will do. He was a murderer from the beginning, and abode not in the truth, because there is no truth in him. When he speaketh a lie, he speaketh of his own: for he is a liar, and the father of it.”

The truth I am writing to you about is not merely the transmission of factual details. The truth I am addressing is the divine truth that belongs exclusively to God.

The joyful reality is that this truth has been made available to us through Jesus Christ. As 2 Corinthians 10:3–4 states:

“For though we walk in the flesh, we do not war after the flesh: (For the weapons of our warfare are not carnal, but mighty through God to the pulling down of strong holds;).”

The Faithfulness of God and the Nature of Covenant

One of the fundamental truths you must know about God is His absolute faithfulness. No other being, whether human or spirit, possesses the inherent ability to be perfectly faithful. God is entirely faithful in His dealings with mankind. For instance, He cannot fail to fulfill His promises to you unless you fail to do your part; this is precisely how a covenant with God operates.

A covenant is a binding agreement between two or more parties in which each is committed to specific responsibilities. In a covenant relationship, every participant has a role to fulfill. God may come to you through a prophet to direct your actions, promising to perform a great work in your life and destiny. He may communicate this to you through dreams, or in any other manner He chooses.

He may say, “Follow me, and I will make you great.” Many people misinterpret this, assuming God merely wants them engaged in endless church activities or rigid religious doctrines. They fail to realize that God desires a personal relationship. They do not understand that it is during the process of following Him that He trains you for the greatness He has planned. This training molds you into the image of Christ, because it takes the grace of God to sustain true greatness.

Many people misunderstand greatness altogether. When God speaks of making you great, He is not referring to mere money, wealth, or material riches. If that is your primary motivation for following Him, your orientation needs to change.

To truly understand divine greatness, let us look at Genesis 12:1–3:

“Now the Lord had said unto Abram, Get thee out of thy country, and from thy kindred, and from thy father's house, unto a land that I will shew thee: And I will make of thee a great nation, and I will bless thee, and make thy name great; and thou shalt be a blessing: And I will bless them that bless thee, and curse him that curseth thee: and in thee shall all families of the earth be blessed.”

If you study Genesis 12, you will notice a distinct progression in Abraham's journey with God. Despite God’s grand promises, Abraham was initially consumed with the immediate desire for a biological child to inherit his estate. In Genesis 15:2–3, he questioned God, noting that he remained childless and that his sole heir was Eliezer of Damascus.

The Lord corrected him, declaring that Eliezer would not be his heir, but rather a son proceeding from his own body. The Lord then led Abraham outside his tent and commanded him to look toward the heavens and count the stars. When Abraham found them impossible to number, the Lord told him, “So shall thy seed be.” The Scriptures record: “And he believed in the Lord; and he counted it to him for righteousness.”

In Genesis 17, God initiated the structural making of the man because Abraham was finally prepared for it. The Lord appeared to him and commanded, “Walk before me, and be thou perfect.” In verses 4–5, God established the covenant:

“As for me, behold, my covenant is with thee, and thou shalt be a father of many nations. Neither shall thy name any more be called Abram, but thy name shall be Abraham; for a father of many nations have I made thee.”

The Lord further promised to make him exceedingly fruitful, declaring that nations and kings would descend from him.

Today, we walk in blessing because of the covenant Abraham cut with God. God is actively fulfilling those ancient promises to Abraham through Jesus Christ. Most of us are not native to Israel and possess no natural lineage to Abraham. Yet, Jesus Christ died explicitly so that the blessing of Abraham might extend to the Gentiles through Him.

Consequently, anyone who accepts Jesus Christ becomes a seed of Abraham. By default, that individual inherits the spiritual seed of greatness. If you have not yet given your life to Jesus Christ, you can do so right now. Romans 10:6–9 explains:

“But the righteousness which is of faith speaketh on this wise, Say not in thine heart, Who shall ascend into heaven? (that is, to bring Christ down from above:) Or, Who shall descend into the deep? (that is, to bring up Christ again from the dead.) But what saith it? The word is nigh thee, even in thy mouth, and in thy heart: that is, the word of faith, which we preach; That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved.”

You can follow me in this prayer right now:

“Lord Jesus, you are my Lord. I believe in my heart that God raised you from the dead.”

Congratulations! You are now a child of God and a seed of Abraham. By accepting Christ into your life as your personal Lord, you possess everlasting life.

This illustrates the true meaning of faithfulness. This is the unyielding faithfulness of God, an attribute no human being can replicate. Abraham fulfilled his part through the righteousness of faith, and God continues to raise up sons unto Abraham to this day.

When Abraham walked the earth, his greatness was undeniable. When his nephew Lot was captured alongside his wealth and the people of his region, Abraham mobilized 318 trained servants born in his own house. They routed a coalition of four nations and recovered all the plundered wealth.

Upon his return, he met Melchizedek, the King of Salem and priest of the Most High God. Melchizedek declared, “Blessed be the most high God, possessor of heaven and earth,” and likewise blessed Abraham. Abraham’s relationship with God reached such a profound depth that he shared relational space with the Creator. God openly called him His friend.

This is the standard of God's faithfulness. He does not fail, and He never breaks a covenant.

A human being can promise and fail. Evil spirits and the powers of darkness habitually promise and fail. Even when a person’s failure to keep a promise is unintentional, the promise remains broken. This is why the Scripture warns against putting ultimate trust in man. It underscores why there is no other God beside Him; He is the only true God. He commands all of creation, yet He cannot be commanded by anything.

Truth as a Weapon of Deliverance

The Scriptures command us to put on the whole armor of God. When you put on truth, your life becomes a witness that inspires others to trust God.

Jesus said, “I and my Father are one,” and “No man commeth unto the Father, but by me.” The moment you accepted Jesus Christ as your personal Savior, He and the Father came to make their abode inside you. You are in Jesus, Jesus is in the Father, and the Father is in Jesus.

Truth is a weapon of deliverance. To put it on means to actively deploy it.

In Daniel 6, we see how Daniel’s unwavering faithfulness to the Most High God compelled King Darius to issue a global decree. The king wrote to all people, nations, and languages dwelling across the earth:

“Peace be multiplied unto you. I make a decree, That in every dominion of my kingdom men tremble and fear before the God of Daniel: for he is the living God, and stedfast for ever, and his kingdom that which shall not be destroyed, and his dominion shall be even unto the end. He delivereth and rescueth, and he worketh signs and wonders in heaven and in earth, who hath delivered Daniel from the power of the lions.”

This decree was issued because Daniel had distinguished himself above all other presidents and princes due to the excellent spirit within him, causing the king to consider setting him over the entire realm (Daniel 6:3).

Predictably, the presidents and princes sought to find an occasion of fault against Daniel regarding his administration of the kingdom. However, they could find no error or fault because he was entirely faithful (Daniel 6:4).

Realizing this, his detractors concluded, “We shall not find any occasion against this Daniel, except we find it against him concerning the law of his God.” They conspired, approached the king, and said, “King Darius, live forever! All the presidents of the kingdom, the governors, the princes, the counselors, and the captains have consulted together to establish a royal statute and to make a firm decree, that whosoever shall ask a petition of any God or man for thirty days, save of thee, O king, he shall be cast into the den of lions.” They urged the king to sign the decree into law so that it could not be altered, according to the unchangeable law of the Medes and Persians. King Darius signed the document.

When Daniel learned that the decree had been signed into law, he did not alter his behavior. He went into his house, and with his chamber windows open toward Jerusalem, he kneeled upon his knees three times a day, prayed, and gave thanks before his God, just as he had always done.

Daniel remained faithful because he feared God and possessed deep knowledge of Him. Proverbs 9:10 reminds us: “The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom: and the knowledge of the holy is understanding.” To fear God means to keep His commandments, and the knowledge of God provides true understanding. Daniel 11:32b echoes this: “…but the people that do know their God shall be strong, and do exploits.”

These are the two dimensions required to effectively wield the weapon of truth:

  1. The Fear of God: Which empowers you to keep His commandments.
  2. The Knowledge of God: Which means understanding exactly who He is.

The Bible records that no fault could be found in Daniel. His enemies tested him in every conceivable way, yet he refused to sin against God.

Your level of dominion on this earth is directly determined by your capacity to fear the Lord and observe His commandments. The Bible explicitly states that God is holy. Many individuals deceive themselves and others by arguing that because Jesus died for us, we can sin freely and simply ask for forgiveness. While it is true that He forgives, you cannot establish the kingdom of God while living in perpetual compromise.

We must allow God to train us so that His divine character directly influences our own. Without this character transformation, we will carry no weight in the spirit realm. Remember, we are actively contending against evil spirits, princes, rulers, and spiritual hosts of wickedness.

You have been called unto freedom, but you must never use your freedom as an incentive, opportunity, or excuse for the flesh. You are called to a high spiritual work. Your spirit was born anew specifically to enable you to walk in the Spirit and render you incapable of gratifying the desires of the flesh.

The works of the flesh are entirely clear, and you must allow God to deliver you from them before you can successfully pull down external demonic strongholds. These works include: immorality, impurity, indecency, idolatry, sorcery, enmity, strife, jealousy, anger (ill temper), selfishness, divisions (dissensions), party spirit (factions, sects with peculiar opinions, heresies), envy, drunkenness, and carousing (Galatians 5:19–21).

Because the fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom, we must recognize that these works of the flesh are sin, and the devil is the father of sin. If you are actively living in sin, you will lack the spiritual authority to rule over Satan and his angels.

The Cost of Disobedience

Sin can cause God to withdraw His active fellowship from His own people. When Jesus carried the sins of the world on the cross, God temporarily withdrew His operational association with Him. By spiritual law, when an unrepentant sinner dies, they descend into Hades—the place of the dead, characterized by fire and torment, functioning much like an earthly prison.

Jesus descended into Hades on our behalf, fulfilling Acts 2:25. Prophetically, David revealed what Jesus declared regarding this descent:

“I foresaw the Lord always before my face, for he is on my right hand, that I should not be moved: Therefore did my heart rejoice, and my tongue was glad; moreover also my flesh shall rest in hope: Because thou wilt not leave my soul in hell, neither wilt thou suffer thine Holy One to see corruption. Thou hast made known to me the ways of life; thou shalt make me full of joy with thy countenance.”

Hades remains the spiritual destination for those who die in sin. It is immaterial whether a person merely claims a nominal belief in Jesus; sin originates from the devil. The moment a person chooses sin and fails to reconcile with God through Christ, they place themselves in severe spiritual jeopardy. If they die in that state, they go to the place of the dead.

The Scriptures explicitly warn that those who practice the works of the flesh will not inherit the kingdom of God (Galatians 5:21). Do not be deceived: whatever a man sows, that he will also reap. He who sows to his flesh will from the flesh reap destruction, but he who sows to the Spirit will from the Spirit reap life everlasting (Galatians 6:7–8).

When you truly believe in Jesus Christ, you possess eternal life because eternal life resides within Christ, and Christ resides directly inside your spirit. However, persistent, unrepented sin paralyzes your spiritual authority and compromises your fellowship with God.

Following your initial repentance, God systematically teaches and trains you so that you no longer conform to the patterns of this world. He equips you for spiritual work. Operating in the Spirit is a form of spiritual sowing. Wise believers invest heavily in the Spirit. Conversely, investing in the flesh yields destruction.

By "investing in the flesh," I mean living life entirely on your own terms, completely ignoring the prescriptions and boundaries set forth in God's Holy Word. A person's lifestyle may look perfectly acceptable, upright, and right in the eyes of human society, yet still be entirely carnal. Wise individuals invest strictly in eternity. No human being or institution can secure assets for you in eternity; only the Most High God holds that capacity.

Jesus confidently declared, "The prince of this world cometh, and hath nothing in me." Because there was no legal hook of sin for the enemy to exploit, Jesus operated in absolute power, performing signs, wonders, and delivering those held captive by the devil.

We must submit to His purification process. He is entirely ready to purify us; our responsibility is simply to listen to His voice and execute His Word. You put on God's armor through active obedience. You must allow God’s nature to thoroughly influence your character. This is the only way to walk in genuine holiness and truth.

This requires spiritual discernment: knowing where you should and should not be, monitoring what you allow your eyes to watch, and guarding your actions. Furthermore, remember that corrupt company ruins good character. Do not make intimate friends out of willfully sinful people. You can interact with them, do business with them, and show them Christ, but do not allow them into your inner circle of friendship. Proverbs 4:23 admonishes: “Keep thy heart with all diligence; for out of it are the issues of life,” because as a man thinks in his heart, so is he.

Sin can cause God to actively resist or oppose your actions. In 1 Chronicles 21, we see that Satan stood up against Israel and provoked David to number the people. David commanded Joab and the rulers of the people, “Go, number Israel from Beer-sheba even to Dan; and bring the number of them to me, that I may know it.”

Joab strongly protested, saying, “The Lord make his people an hundred times so many more as they be: but, my lord the king, are they not all my lord's servants? why then doth my lord require this thing? why will he be a cause of trespass to Israel?”

Nevertheless, the king’s word prevailed against Joab. Joab traveled throughout all Israel and returned to Jerusalem with the census data. God was deeply displeased with this prideful action; therefore, He smote Israel.

David immediately recognized his error, confessed his sin, and begged God to take away his iniquity. The Lord dispatched the prophet Gad, David’s seer, with a message: “Go and tell David, saying, Thus saith the Lord, I offer thee three things: choose thee one of them, that I may do it unto thee.”

Gad approached David and presented the options: three years of famine, three months of military devastation and defeat by his enemies, or three days of the sword of the Lord, manifesting as a deadly pestilence executed by the angel of the Lord throughout the territory of Israel. Gad asked, “Advise thyself what word I shall bring again to him that sent me.”

David replied, “I am in a great strait: let me fall now into the hand of the Lord; for very great are his mercies: but let me not fall into the hand of man.”

Consequently, the Lord sent a pestilence upon Israel, and seventy thousand men fell. A careful reading of this historical account reveals that while Satan initiated the temptation, the legal vulnerability created by David's compliance resulted in severe divine judgment.

Sin can sever your spiritual protection, authorizing Satan to destroy your physical body and send your soul to Hades. God may even cut a life short if an individual becomes a persistent obstacle to His divine will.

Ecclesiastes 12:13–14 summarizes this truth flawlessly:

“Let us hear the conclusion of the whole matter: Fear God, and keep his commandments: for this is the whole duty of man. For God shall bring every work into judgment, with every secret thing, whether it be good, or whether it be evil.”

If you want to successfully confront the wiles of the devil and win, you must master specific, foundational truths: primarily, a deep personal knowledge of God, paired with a thorough understanding of His written Word and the prophetic declarations concerning your life, your family, your community, and your nation.

Cultivating the Knowledge of God

Let us return to the account of Daniel. When the unalterable law of the Medes and Persians was officially signed by King Darius, Daniel’s deep knowledge of God’s supreme character empowered him to completely look past the royal decree. His immediate response was to go home, open his windows toward Jerusalem, and pray three times a day, just as he had done previously.

Daniel understood exactly who God was. His actions demonstrated a clear conviction: no earthly decree signed by a human king could dictate or disrupt his service to the Creator. He knew that God sits high above all created things, and that obedience to God must always supersede compliance with human mandates.

His enemies, watching for his downfall, found Daniel praying and making supplication before his God. They hurried to the king and reported, “Daniel, which is of the children of the captivity of Judah, regardeth not thee, O king, nor the decree that thou hast signed, but maketh his petition three times a day.”

Upon hearing this, King Darius was deeply distressed. He valued Daniel highly and labored intensely until sunset, trying to find a legal loophole to rescue him.

Those administrators had craftily presented the decree under the guise of honoring the king, masking their true, murderous intent to eliminate Daniel.

When it became clear that the king was delaying the execution order, the conspirators returned, reminding him that under the law of the Medes and Persians, no decree signed by the king could be altered. Left with no choice, the king reluctantly commanded that Daniel be brought out and cast into the den of lions. Yet, even as Daniel was lowered, the king declared to him, “Thy God whom thou servest continually, he will deliver thee.”

The king returned to his palace, spent the night fasting, refused all entertainment, and was unable to sleep. Very early the next morning, he rushed to the lions' den. Approaching the opening, he cried out in anguish, “O Daniel, servant of the living God, is thy God, whom thou servest continually, able to deliver thee from the lions?”

Daniel answered clearly, “O king, live forever! My God hath sent his angel, and hath shut the lions' mouths, that they have not hurt me: forasmuch as before him innocency was found in me; and also before thee, O king, have I done no hurt.”

Overjoyed, the king commanded that Daniel be brought up out of the den. When he was lifted out, no injury of any kind was found on his body because he completely believed in his God. Daniel’s faith was not a blind presumption; it was rooted in a track record of knowing God intimately as a deliverer.

The king then commanded that Daniel’s accusers, along with their wives and children, be cast into the very same den. Before they ever reached the bottom of the pit, the lions overpowered them and broke their bones to pieces.

Following this event, King Darius issued a new declaration to every language on earth:

“Peace be multiplied unto you. I make a decree, That in every dominion of my kingdom men tremble and fear before the God of Daniel: for he is the living God, and steadfast for ever, and his kingdom that which shall not be destroyed, and his dominion shall be even unto the end. He delivereth and rescueth, and he worketh signs and wonders in heaven and in earth, who hath delivered Daniel from the power of the lions.”

Daniel continued to prosper through the reign of Cyrus the Persian. By utilizing the weapon of truth, he completely shattered the complex wiles of the devil. His innocence and blamelessness allowed God to bear witness to his life. Because of his stance, the knowledge of God spread across the known world, and countless individuals learned to trust the Lord.

The Work of the Holy Spirit

I urge you to study your Bible deeply. Learn God’s nature, His character, and His power; make that your starting point. You must know the God you serve. Psalms 103 and 104 are excellent starting points for studying His attributes. In Philippians 3:10, Paul explicitly stated that his ultimate, determined purpose was to know Him.

There are two distinct types of knowledge: intellectual knowledge and experiential knowledge. You begin by learning who God is through the written Scriptures, and as you do, He steps in to grant you a direct, personal experience of Himself. Despite His immense greatness and mystery, God deeply desires to be known by us.

Ultimately, you must allow the Holy Spirit to lead you. He is the ultimate Revealer, Teacher, and Comforter. Jesus assured His disciples that when the Holy Spirit came, He would teach them all things. This arrangement works entirely to our benefit.

The Holy Spirit is the one who interprets the deep mysteries of this world for us. He functions as our internal wisdom. When we speak, we do not speak merely from human observation or limited intellect; we speak by His direct teaching and divine wisdom. He is the very wisdom of God alive within us.

The Holy Spirit is also the distributor of the Word. John 1:1 reveals that Jesus is the Word—the exact Word used to frame the heavens, the earth, and everything within them. This is the very Word that the Holy Spirit distributes and seals within our hearts.

When you are genuinely led by the Holy Spirit, you carry more spiritual authority than any earthly or heavenly power. The Holy Spirit unlocks the Scriptures so you can know Jesus, the living Word, revealing the deep nature and character of the Father.

It takes the dynamic combination of the Holy Spirit and the Word of God to produce anything of tangible spiritual value. Because Christ is the resurrection and the life, when you speak under the direct prompting of the Holy Spirit, even dead situations are brought back to life.

Hebrews 11:3 explains: “Through faith we understand that the worlds were framed by the word of god, so that things which are seen were not made of things which do appear.” When God the Father speaks, the Holy Spirit moves out and utilizes the agency of God the Son to frame the cosmos. The three operate as one.

In John 6:63, Jesus stated: “…the words that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life.” When He spoke, the Holy Spirit and life (which is Christ Himself) went forth to minister. This is the exact spiritual blueprint for how we are called to live and operate on this earth today.
