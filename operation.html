Lagos, Ogun Police Commands Rescue Five Abducted Victims, Arrest 88 Suspects in Massive Forests Raid

Joint security forces launch "Operation Kosaye" across state boundaries, neutralizing four kidnappers and recovering caches of illegal firearms.
By Chukwuma Akhimien | Crime Correspondent

Operatives of the Lagos and Ogun State Police Commands have recorded a major breakthrough against insecurity following a sweeping, joint anti-crime operation codenamed “Operation Kosaye”. The aggressive bush-combing exercise across thick forests, shanties, and boundary criminal hideouts resulted in the rescue of five kidnapped victims, the neutralization of four armed kidnappers, and the arrest of 88 suspects.

Addressing newsmen on Saturday at the Ogun Police Training College in Iperu, the Ogun State Commissioner of Police, Bode Ojajuni, stated that the operation was birthed under the direct initiative of the Inspector-General of Police, Olatunji Disu, with total deployment support from the governors of both Lagos and Ogun States. The Yoruba-coined term "Kosaye" directly translates to "no chance" or "no space" for criminal activity.

Operation Kosaye Breakdown
Out of the 88 suspects currently in police custody, 84 individuals (consisting of 75 males and 9 females) were rounded up during the general perimeter sweep of forest hideouts. An additional four suspects were captured alive during a fierce gun duel in the specific rescue mission.

According to tactical reports, the tactical rescue unit swung into action following a severe security breach along the J3–J4 corridor on the Benin-Sagamu Expressway. Armed operatives successfully intercepted a ring of bandits who had abducted five travelers along the Ore–Ogbere road earlier on Thursday. In the ensuing confrontation, four kidnappers were fatally neutralized, while another four were subdued and taken into custody.

The extensive combing exercise successfully cleared out vast stretches of notorious border terrains, navigating areas from Ikeja, the Kara Bridge, the Redeemed Camp, and the Isheri corridor, down into Owode Egba, Ogere, Ijebu Igbo, Ijebu Ode, and Epe. Caches of weapons including AK-47 rifles, pump-action guns, English and locally-made pistols, battle axes, cutlasses, and mobile devices used for ransom negotiation were recovered.

Lagos State Commissioner of Police, Tijani Fatai, strongly debunked lingering public rumors that heavily armed bandits had successfully infiltrated and taken over the forests of the South-West corridors. Reassuring residents, Fatai stated that "Operation Kosaye" remains an ongoing, continuous exercise designed to permanently secure the economic arteries of both states.
